Research


Welcome! I’m Seonghui (/səŋˈhiː/) Lee, a PhD candidate in the Department of Political Science at Rice University. For the 2014-15 academic year, I am a pre-doctoral research fellow in the Department of Political Science at the University of Zurich, Switzerland.


I study Comparative Politics and Political Behavior with a primary focus on how political contexts influence political behavior. As my research links macro-level political contexts to micro-level political behavior, my interests touch on various themes in political psychology, public opinion, political institutions, and elections and parties. My dissertation research focuses on political interest and knowledge. I propose a theoretical framework that draws on appraisal models of interest in psychology; but further extend the theory to explain why political interest and knowledge vary so dramatically across countries. I empirically test the proposed appraisal structure of political interest with lab, online, and survey experiments. I also use large-N survey data and media contents analysis to validate the main implication from the proposed cross-national theory, i.e., how contextually generated information shortcuts (or heuristics) influence cross-national differences in political interest and knowledge. This project is supported by a NSF Doctoral Dissertation Improvement grant.


I have several other research projects including the cross-national comparability of survey instruments, conceptualization and measurement issues in political knowledge, the nature of party attachment in multiparty systems, the participation marriage gap in Switzerland, the political knowledge gender gap in Western European countries, political dissatisfaction and its electoral consequences in plurality systems, economic voting in East Asian countries, and the newspaper contents analysis on hydraulic fracking in the US. My work on party responsiveness in presidential countries is forthcoming in Comparative Political Studies.
